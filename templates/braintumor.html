About Brain Tumors

A brain tumor is an abnormal growth of cells in the brain that can be either benign (non-cancerous) or malignant (cancerous). Early detection significantly improves treatment outcomes.

Tumor Types

Primary brain tumors originate in the brain, while metastatic tumors spread from cancer in other organs.

Diagnosis

MRI and CT scans are the primary diagnostic tools, often followed by biopsy for confirmation.

Common Symptoms

Recognizing these symptoms can lead to earlier detection:

  • Headaches

    New or worsening headaches, often worse in the morning

  • Seizures

    New onset of seizures without previous history

  • Vision Problems

    Blurred vision, double vision, or loss of peripheral vision

  • Speech Difficulties

    Trouble speaking or understanding speech

  • Balance Issues

    Problems with coordination or balance
